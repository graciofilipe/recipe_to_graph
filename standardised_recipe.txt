```
Ingredients:

Vegetables and Herbs:
* 1 large onion, finely chopped
* 2 celery stalks (with leaves if possible), finely chopped
* 4 cloves garlic, minced
* 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
* 1/4 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
* Lemon wedges, for serving

Spices and Seasoning:
* 1 tbsp olive oil
* 1 tsp ground ginger
* 1 tsp ground turmeric
* 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
* 1/2 tsp black pepper
* 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
* Salt to taste

Canned and Dry Goods:
* 1 (14.5 ounce / 411g) can crushed tomatoes
* 2 tbsp tomato paste
* 1 (15-ounce / 425g) can lentils, drained and rinsed
* 1 (15-ounce / 425g) can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
* 6-8 cups vegetable broth (or chicken broth, low sodium preferred)
* 1/4 cup long grain rice

Steps:

Saut√©ing Aromatics:
1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
2. Add the chopped onion and celery to the pot and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.

Blooming Spices:
3. Add the minced garlic, ginger, turmeric, cinnamon, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using) to the pot.
4. Cook for about 1 minute, stirring constantly, until fragrant.

Combining and Simmering:
5. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, drained lentils, drained chickpeas, and vegetable broth to the pot.
6. Stir well to combine.
7. Add the rice to the pot.
8. Bring the soup to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low.
9. Cover the pot and simmer gently for 20-25 minutes, or until the flavors have melded and the rice is fully cooked.

Finishing and Serving:
10. Stir in the chopped cilantro and parsley.
11. Serve hot, garnished with lemon wedges.

Preparation (Can be done in parallel with other steps):
1. Drain and rinse lentils.
2. Drain and rinse chickpeas.
3. Chop Cilantro and Parsley.
```
